## 2.2 Equity: Owning a Piece of Businesses

### What is Equity?
When you buy equity (stocks), you become a **part-owner** of the company. Your wealth grows when the company grows.

### Types of Stocks

| Category | Market Cap | Characteristics | Risk Level | Expected Return |
|----------|------------|-----------------|------------|-----------------|
| **Large Cap** | Top 100 companies | Stable, established, dividend-paying | Medium | 10-14% |
| **Mid Cap** | 101-250 companies | Growth phase, moderate stability | Medium-High | 12-18% |
| **Small Cap** | 251+ companies | High growth potential, volatile | High | 15-25%+ |
| **Blue Chip** | Industry leaders | Very stable, consistent growth | Low-Medium | 10-12% |

### Ways to Invest in Equity

#### 1. Direct Stocks
**Pros:**
- Complete control over selection
- No management fees
- Potential for high returns

**Cons:**
- Requires research & expertise
- Higher risk if not diversified
- Emotional decision-making

**Best For:** Experienced investors with time for research

#### 2. Mutual Funds (Actively Managed)
**Pros:**
- Professional management
- Instant diversification
- Systematic investment (SIP)

**Cons:**
- Expense ratio (1-2.5%)
- Most underperform index
- Exit loads

**Best For:** Most individual investors

#### 3. Index Funds & ETFs (Passively Managed)
**Pros:**
- Very low expense ratio (0.1-0.5%)
- Market returns guaranteed
- No fund manager risk

**Cons:**
- No chance to beat market
- Market downturns hurt

**Best For:** Long-term investors who believe in market efficiency

### Stock Categories by Investment Style

| Style | Definition | Examples | When to Buy |
|-------|------------|----------|-------------|
| **Value** | Undervalued stocks | Banks during crisis | Market pessimism |
| **Growth** | High growth companies | Tech, pharma | Long-term horizon |
| **Dividend** | Regular income | ITC, Coal India | Income investors |
| **Momentum** | Rising stocks | Trending sectors | Short-medium term |
| **Quality** | Strong fundamentals | HDFC, TCS | Always good |

## 2.3 Fixed Income: Lending Your Money

### What is Fixed Income?
When you invest in fixed income, you're **lending money** to governments or companies in exchange for regular interest payments.

### Fixed Income Options Compared

| Instrument | Interest Rate | Lock-in | Tax Treatment | Risk Level |
|------------|---------------|---------|---------------|------------|
| **Savings Account** | 2.5-4% | None | Taxable | Very Low |
| **Fixed Deposit** | 5-7.5% | 7 days - 10 years | Taxable | Very Low |
| **RD (Recurring)** | 5-7% | 6 months - 10 years | Taxable | Very Low |
| **PPF** | 7.1% | 15 years | Tax-free (EEE) | Zero |
| **EPF** | 8.15% | Till retirement | Tax-free | Zero |
| **NSC** | 7.7% | 5 years | Taxable | Zero |
| **Govt Bonds** | 6-8% | Varies | Taxable | Very Low |
| **Corporate Bonds** | 8-12% | Varies | Taxable | Low-Medium |
| **Debt Mutual Funds** | 6-9% | None | Taxable | Low |

### Government Small Saving Schemes (Best Safe Options)

#### PPF (Public Provident Fund)
- **Rate:** 7.1% (compounded annually)
- **Lock-in:** 15 years (partial withdrawal after 7 years)
- **Tax:** EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) - NO TAX at all
- **Limit:** ₹1.5 lakhs/year
- **Best For:** Long-term debt allocation, tax saving

#### NPS (National Pension System)
- **Returns:** 8-10% (market-linked)
- **Tax:** Extra ₹50,000 deduction under 80CCD(1B)
- **Lock-in:** Till 60 (partial withdrawal allowed)
- **Best For:** Retirement planning, tax saving

### Debt Mutual Funds Categories

| Category | Duration | Risk | Suitable For |
|----------|----------|------|--------------|
| **Liquid Fund** | < 91 days | Very Low | Emergency fund, parking money |
| **Ultra Short Duration** | 3-6 months | Low | Short-term goals |
| **Short Duration** | 1-3 years | Low-Medium | 2-3 year goals |
| **Medium Duration** | 3-4 years | Medium | Medium-term goals |
| **Gilt Funds** | 10+ years | Medium | Long-term, govt bonds only |
| **Corporate Bond** | Varies | Medium | Higher returns, credit risk |

## 2.4 Real Assets: Tangible Wealth

### Gold: The Timeless Store of Value

#### Why Gold?
- **Inflation hedge** - Maintains purchasing power
- **Crisis protection** - Performs well in uncertainty
- **Negative correlation** - Often rises when stocks fall
- **Cultural significance** - Important in Indian context

#### Ways to Invest in Gold

| Method | Purity | Storage | Liquidity | Making Charges |
|--------|--------|---------|-----------|----------------|
| **Physical Gold** | Varies | Your responsibility | Medium | 8-25% |
| **Gold ETF** | 99.5% | Demat | High | None |
| **Sovereign Gold Bond** | 99.9% | Certificate | Medium | None + 2.5% interest |
| **Digital Gold** | 99.9% | Trustee | High | Low spread |
| **Gold Mutual Fund** | Via ETF | Demat | High | Expense ratio |

**Winner: Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGB)**
- No storage hassle
- 2.5% annual interest
- Tax-free capital gains if held 8 years
- Government guarantee

#### Recommended Gold Allocation: 5-15% of portfolio

### Real Estate

#### Types of Real Estate Investment

| Type | Investment Required | Liquidity | Returns | Hassle |
|------|---------------------|-----------|---------|--------|
| **Residential** | ₹25L - ₹5Cr+ | Very Low | 2-5% rental + appreciation | High |
| **Commercial** | ₹1Cr+ | Very Low | 6-10% rental + appreciation | Medium |
| **REITs** | ₹10,000+ | High | 6-8% dividend + appreciation | Zero |
| **Real Estate MFs** | ₹500+ | High | Market-linked | Zero |

#### REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts)
**How it works:** Own a piece of premium commercial real estate (malls, offices, warehouses) without buying property.

**Available in India:**
- Embassy Office Parks REIT
- Mindspace Business Parks REIT
- Brookfield India REIT

**Benefits:**
- 90% income distributed as dividends
- Professionally managed
- Highly liquid
- Low ticket size

## 2.6 Investment Vehicles Compared

### Mutual Funds vs ETFs vs Index Funds

| Feature | Mutual Fund (Active) | Index Fund | ETF |
|---------|---------------------|------------|-----|
| **Management** | Active | Passive | Passive |
| **Expense Ratio** | 1-2.5% | 0.1-0.5% | 0.1-0.3% |
| **Trading** | NAV end of day | NAV end of day | Real-time |
| **Min Investment** | ₹500 (SIP) | ₹500 | 1 unit (~₹100-500) |
| **SIP Available** | Yes | Yes | No (mostly) |
| **Returns** | Varies (mostly underperform) | Market returns | Market returns |
| **Demat Required** | No | No | Yes |
| **Best For** | SIP investors | Long-term investors | Active traders |

### The Case for Index Funds

**Shocking Statistics:**
- 80-90% of active mutual funds underperform their benchmark index over 10+ years
- You pay higher fees for lower returns
- Fund managers change, index doesn't

**Warren Buffett's Bet:**
Buffett bet $1 million that an S&P 500 index fund would beat hedge funds over 10 years. **He won by a landslide.**

## Key Takeaways

1. **Equity = Ownership** - Higher risk, higher long-term returns
2. **Fixed Income = Lending** - Stable, predictable, lower returns  
3. **Real Assets = Tangible** - Inflation protection, diversification
4. **Index Funds beat active funds** - Lower cost, market returns
5. **Sovereign Gold Bonds** - Best way to own gold in India
6. **REITs** - Real estate exposure without the hassles
7. **Diversify across asset classes** - Not just within them
