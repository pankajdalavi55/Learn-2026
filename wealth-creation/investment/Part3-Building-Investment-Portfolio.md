## 3.1 The Three Pillars of Portfolio Construction

### Pillar 1: Asset Allocation
**Deciding HOW MUCH to put in each asset class**

Research shows that **90% of portfolio returns** come from asset allocation, not stock picking or market timing.

## 3.2 Age-Based Asset Allocation Strategies

### The Traditional Rule: 100 - Age = Equity %

| Age | Equity % | Debt % | Logic |
|-----|----------|--------|-------|
| 25 | 75% | 25% | Long horizon, can take risk |
| 35 | 65% | 35% | Building wealth phase |
| 45 | 55% | 45% | Peak earning, reducing risk |
| 55 | 45% | 55% | Nearing retirement |
| 65 | 35% | 65% | Capital preservation |

### The Modern Rule: 110 - Age = Equity %
People live longer now, need more growth. Increase equity allocation by 10%.

## 3.5 Diversification Done Right

### Level 1: Across Asset Classes
```
Portfolio
├── Equity (60%)
├── Debt (30%)
├── Gold (10%)
└── Cash (Emergency Fund - separate)
```

### Level 2: Within Asset Classes
```
Equity (60% of portfolio)
├── Indian Large Cap (40% of equity)
├── Indian Mid Cap (25% of equity)
├── Indian Small Cap (15% of equity)
├── International (20% of equity)

Debt (30% of portfolio)
├── PPF (40% of debt)
├── EPF (30% of debt)
├── Debt Mutual Fund (30% of debt)
```

### Level 3: Across Time (Rupee Cost Averaging)
- Invest regularly via SIP
- Avoid timing the market
- Average out purchase price

### Common Diversification Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It's Wrong | Solution |
|---------|----------------|----------|
| **Too many funds** | Overlap, over-diversification | Max 5-7 funds total |
| **Multiple similar funds** | Same stocks, higher cost | One fund per category |
| **Only Indian stocks** | Country concentration risk | Add international exposure |
| **No rebalancing** | Allocation drifts | Annual rebalancing |
| **Timing-based investing** | Emotional decisions | Systematic SIP |

## 3.7 Rebalancing: The Discipline That Pays

### Why Rebalancing Matters
Over time, your portfolio drifts from target allocation. Rebalancing:
- Forces "sell high, buy low"
- Maintains desired risk level
- Removes emotional decision-making

### Example: Annual Rebalancing

**Target Allocation:** 60% Equity, 30% Debt, 10% Gold

| Asset | Jan 2025 | Dec 2025 (Actual) | Drift | Action |
|-------|----------|-------------------|-------|--------|
| Equity | ₹6,00,000 (60%) | ₹8,00,000 (67%) | +7% | Sell ₹84,000 |
| Debt | ₹3,00,000 (30%) | ₹2,80,000 (23%) | -7% | Add ₹76,000 |
| Gold | ₹1,00,000 (10%) | ₹1,20,000 (10%) | 0% | Hold |
| **Total** | ₹10,00,000 | ₹12,00,000 | | |

**After Rebalancing:** ₹7,20,000 Equity | ₹3,60,000 Debt | ₹1,20,000 Gold

### Rebalancing Methods

| Method | Frequency | Best For |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| **Calendar** | Annually/Semi-annually | Most investors |
| **Threshold** | When drift > 5% | Active investors |
| **Cash Flow** | Use new investments | Tax-efficient |
| **Combination** | Annual or if >10% drift | My recommendation |

### Tax-Efficient Rebalancing
1. **Use new investments** to buy underweight assets
2. **Redirect SIPs** temporarily to underweight assets
3. **Avoid selling** if within threshold
4. **Use tax-loss harvesting** when selling

## 3.8 Monitoring Your Portfolio

### What to Review Quarterly
- [ ] Overall portfolio return vs benchmark
- [ ] Individual fund performance
- [ ] Asset allocation drift
- [ ] Any fund manager changes

### What to Review Annually
- [ ] Complete portfolio rebalancing
- [ ] Goal progress tracking
- [ ] Tax harvesting opportunities
- [ ] Life changes (adjust strategy if needed)

### What to IGNORE
- ❌ Daily market movements
- ❌ Short-term underperformance (< 1 year)
- ❌ Market predictions and "tips"
- ❌ What others are investing in

## Key Takeaways

1. **Asset allocation drives 90% of returns** - Get this right first
2. **Match investments with goals** - Timeline determines risk capacity
3. **Use Core-Satellite approach** - Index core + Active satellite
4. **Diversify properly** - Across classes, within classes, across time
5. **Rebalance annually** - Maintains discipline, forces buy low/sell high
6. **Keep it simple** - 5-7 funds maximum
7. **Monitor, don't obsess** - Quarterly check, annual rebalancing
