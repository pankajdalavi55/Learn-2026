## 1. What is Cryptocurrency?

### Definition
Cryptocurrency is a **digital or virtual currency** that uses cryptography for security and operates on a decentralized network called **blockchain**.

### Key Characteristics

```
CRYPTOCURRENCY FUNDAMENTALS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

DECENTRALIZED
├── No central authority (bank/government)
├── Peer-to-peer network
└── Global, borderless transactions

CRYPTOGRAPHIC
├── Secured by complex mathematics
├── Virtually impossible to counterfeit
└── Transparent yet pseudonymous

IMMUTABLE
├── Once recorded, cannot be changed
├── Complete transaction history
└── Trustless system

LIMITED SUPPLY (Most)
├── Bitcoin: 21 million max
├── Built-in scarcity
└── Deflationary by design

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

## 2. How Blockchain Works

### The Technology Behind Crypto

```
BLOCKCHAIN EXPLAINED (Simplified)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Traditional Banking:
You → Bank (Central Authority) → Recipient
     [Bank verifies & records]

Blockchain:
You → Network of Computers → Recipient
     [Everyone verifies & records]

STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS:

1. TRANSACTION INITIATED
   └── Arjun wants to send 1 BTC to Priya

2. TRANSACTION BROADCAST
   └── Sent to network of thousands of computers (nodes)

3. VALIDATION
   └── Nodes verify: Does Arjun have 1 BTC? Is signature valid?

4. BLOCK CREATION
   └── Valid transactions grouped into a "block"

5. CONSENSUS
   └── Network agrees block is valid (Proof of Work/Stake)

6. BLOCK ADDED
   └── New block linked to previous blocks (chain)

7. TRANSACTION COMPLETE
   └── Priya receives 1 BTC; recorded forever

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

### Consensus Mechanisms

| Mechanism | How It Works | Used By | Energy Use |
|-----------|--------------|---------|------------|
| **Proof of Work (PoW)** | Miners solve complex puzzles | Bitcoin, Litecoin | Very High |
| **Proof of Stake (PoS)** | Validators stake coins | Ethereum, Cardano | Low |
| **Delegated PoS** | Elected validators | EOS, Tron | Very Low |
| **Proof of History** | Cryptographic timestamps | Solana | Low |

## 8. Risk Management for Crypto

### The Golden Rules

```
CRYPTO RISK MANAGEMENT RULES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

RULE 1: POSITION SIZING
├── Maximum 5-10% of total portfolio in crypto
├── For ₹500 Cr goal: Max ₹25-50 Cr in crypto
└── Never bet the farm on crypto

RULE 2: DIVERSIFICATION
├── 50-60% in Bitcoin (safest)
├── 25-30% in Ethereum (smart contracts leader)
├── 10-20% in altcoins (higher risk)
└── Never >5% in any single altcoin

RULE 3: NEVER INVEST BORROWED MONEY
├── No margin trading
├── No crypto loans for buying more crypto
└── Leverage kills in volatile markets

RULE 4: USE STOP-LOSSES (for active trading)
├── Set stop-loss at 15-20% below entry
├── Move stop-loss up as price rises
└── Accept small losses to avoid big ones

RULE 5: SECURE YOUR HOLDINGS
├── Hardware wallet for long-term holdings
├── 2FA on all accounts
├── Never share seed phrase
├── Use unique passwords
└── Beware of phishing

RULE 6: HAVE AN EXIT PLAN
├── Define profit targets before buying
├── Take profits gradually, not all at once
├── Never fall in love with a position
└── Profits aren't real until realized

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```
